Posture Tips

  • Keep ears aligned over shoulders — each inch forward adds ~10 lbs of neck load.
  • Engage your core slightly to protect the lumbar spine during lifting.
  • When lifting, squat — not bend. Keep the load close to your centre of mass.
  • Switch seated positions every 30 minutes to reduce disc compression.
  • A balanced CoM directly over both feet reduces fall risk to near zero.

How the Physics Engine Works

FitCore uses a custom 2D Verlet Integration engine — the same approach used in game physics and biomechanical research software.

Verlet Integration

  • Each joint is a Point with position, previous position, and mass.
  • Velocity is implicit: v = (x − x_prev) × damping.
  • Acceleration from gravity + external forces is added each frame.
  • Energy is conserved naturally — no explicit velocity accumulation.

Constraint Solving

  • Bones are rigid distance constraints — maintain segment length.
  • Angle constraints limit joint range of motion (e.g. knees can't hyperextend).
  • Muscles are spring actuators — they can only pull, not push.
  • 12 iterations per frame ensures stability without explosion.

Telemetry & Strain

  • Cervical angle = head forward displacement relative to neck axis.
  • Lumbar torque = Σ(mass × 9.81 × horizontal moment arm) at L4/L5.
  • CoM projected onto ground — if outside foot base, balance is lost.
  • Muscle strain = spring tension ÷ max isometric force capacity.
